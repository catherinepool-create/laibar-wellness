I used to wake up stiff. Not "old person" stiff—I'm in my 30s—but that creaky, takes-20-minutes-to-feel-normal stiff.

Then I built a morning routine around joint mobility. Five minutes. Non-negotiable. Here's what changed.

## The Routine

**Minute 1: Ankle Circles (in bed)**
Before you even stand up, rotate each ankle 10 times clockwise, 10 times counter-clockwise. This gets synovial fluid moving in your feet and ankles—the joints that take the most abuse.

**Minute 2: Knee Hugs**
Lying on your back, pull one knee to your chest. Hold 15 seconds. Switch. Repeat. This gently mobilizes your hips and lower back without loading your knees.

**Minute 3: Cat-Cow (on all fours)**
The classic yoga move. Arch your back, drop your belly, look up. Then round your spine, tuck your chin, push through your hands. 10 slow cycles. This wakes up your entire spine.

**Minute 4: Shoulder Circles**
Standing now. Big, slow circles—forward 10 times, backward 10 times. Focus on the full range. Most people's shoulders are locked up from sleeping and screens.

**Minute 5: Wrist Rotations + Finger Spreads**
Circle your wrists. Spread your fingers wide, then make fists. Repeat 10 times. Your hands do more work than you realize—give them attention.

## Why It Works

Joint health isn't just about supplements (though those help). It's about **movement quality**.

Synovial fluid—the lubricant inside your joints—doesn't circulate when you're still. It needs motion to distribute. That's why you feel stiff after sleeping: your joints have been dry for 7+ hours.

> The morning routine is basically an oil change for your body.

## Make It Stick

**Pair it with something you already do.** I do the routine right after I turn off my alarm, before checking my phone. No decisions required.

**Don't optimize too early.** Five minutes, done daily, beats 30 minutes done sporadically.

**Track it.** A simple habit tracker—just checkboxes. Seeing the streak grow is motivating.

## The Takeaway

You don't need an hour at the gym to support your joints. You need consistency.

Five minutes. Every morning. Combine it with the right supplement, and you'll forget you ever had a "stiff phase."
