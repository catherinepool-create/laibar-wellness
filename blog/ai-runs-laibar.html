What Happens When You Run a Wellness Brand With Zero Employees

March 19, 2026  •  7 min read

← Back to The Journal

People assume Laibar has a team. A marketing person. A customer service rep. Maybe a formulation consultant.

Nope. Laibar runs with five AI agents and one human (me). No employees. No freelancers. No contractors. Here's how it actually works.

The Squad

Every agent has a defined role, a personality framework, and a scope of work. They don't overlap. They coordinate through a shared mission queue.

TitanDirector. Reviews deliverables, assigns tasks, escalates blockers, runs the morning brief.
LuxCreative lead. Brand voice, visual direction, copy approval. Nothing ships without Lux's sign-off.
RexResearch. Monitors clinical studies, verifies ingredient claims, watches competitor moves.
AxiomGrowth. Pricing strategy, channel analysis, conversion optimization, marketplace listings.
MavenCustomer experience. Handles questions, processes escalations, designs post-purchase rituals.

A Typical Day

6:00 AM

Titan runs the morning review. Any stuck tasks get escalated. A daily brief lands in my inbox — priorities, blockers, wins from yesterday.

8:00 AM

I review the brief. Adjust priorities if needed. This takes about 10 minutes. Then I hand direction back to the squad.

Throughout the day

Agents work the queue. Rex might flag a new boswellia study. Lux drafts three product caption variants. Maven handles a shipping question before I even see it.

11:00 PM

Nightly build. Content scheduled, deliverables wrapped, anything needing human review flagged for morning. I wake up with it done.

Why This Works

  • AI doesn't forget. Every agent has access to the same memory files — brand guidelines, customer FAQs, ingredient specs. No re-briefing required.
  • AI doesn't get tired. Agents check the queue on a schedule. They don't burn out or take mental health days.
  • Consistency is the floor, not the ceiling. Lux doesn't have off days where copy comes out sloppy. Rex doesn't skip a study because it looked boring.

What Still Needs a Human

Let's be clear: I haven't automated myself out of my own business.

  • Strategy — which products to launch, which channels to prioritize, when to pivot
  • Taste — final approval on creative, tone, anything customer-facing
  • Relationships — suppliers, partners, anyone who wants to talk to "the founder"

The agents handle execution. I handle judgment.

The Economics

Traditional supplement brand: 10–15 people minimum. Salaries, benefits, management overhead.

Laibar: a few hundred dollars/month in API costs + my time. That delta goes straight into better ingredients.
