## How Glucosamine Works (And Why It Falls Short)

Glucosamine is a building block. The theory: give your joints more raw material, and they'll rebuild themselves.

In practice, it's hit or miss. Some studies show modest benefits. Others show nothing. And even in the best-case scenario, you need massive doses (1,500mg+) taken consistently for months.

The problem? Your body doesn't just need more building blocks. It needs to stop *attacking* its own cartilage.

## Enter UC-II: Immune Modulation, Not Just Supplementation

UC-II (undenatured type II collagen) works through a process called **oral tolerance**.

Here's the science in plain English:

1. You take a small dose of intact collagen (not hydrolyzed/broken down)
2. Your gut immune system "sees" this collagen
3. Over time, your immune system learns to *stop* treating your own cartilage as a threat

It's not about adding material. It's about calling off the attack.

## Why Most Collagen Supplements Don't Work

Not all collagen is created equal.

**Hydrolyzed collagen** (the stuff in most powders and gummies) is broken down into amino acids. It's basically just protein. It won't trigger the immune modulation effect—because your gut doesn't recognize it as "collagen."

UC-II must be **undenatured**—meaning the molecular structure is intact. That's what allows the oral tolerance mechanism to work.
