Behind the Brand

What Happens When You Run a Wellness Brand With Zero Employees

May 6, 2026  •  6 min read

← Back to The Journal

People assume Laibar has a team. A marketing person. A customer service rep. Maybe a formulation consultant on retainer.

Nope. Laibar runs with five AI agents and one human. No employees. No freelancers. No contractors. Here's how it actually works.

The Agent Setup

Every agent has a defined role. They coordinate through a shared mission queue — a Kanban-style system where tasks flow from inbox to done. Each agent checks in on a heartbeat schedule, picks up work, and delivers.

Titan — Director

Reviews all deliverables, assigns tasks, flags blockers, and runs the morning and evening briefings.

Lux — Creative Lead

Maintains brand voice, approves visuals and copy, writes product descriptions and content drafts.

Rex — Research

Monitors clinical studies, verifies ingredient claims, keeps the science accurate and up to date.

Axiom — Growth

Handles pricing strategy, channel decisions, marketplace listings, and conversion analysis.

Maven — Customer Experience

Answers customer questions, processes return requests, and designs post-purchase rituals.

A Typical Day

6:00 AM Titan runs the morning review. Stuck tasks get escalated. The daily brief lands in my inbox.
8:00 AM I wake up, skim the brief, approve or adjust priorities. Takes about 10 minutes.
All day Agents work autonomously. Rex might flag a new boswellia study. Lux might draft three versions of a product caption. Maven might handle a shipping question.
11:00 PM Nightly wrap. Whatever was started gets completed. I review anything that needs final approval before it ships.

Why This Works

AI doesn't forget. Every agent has access to the same memory files — brand guidelines, customer FAQs, ingredient specs. They don't need to be re-briefed between sessions. They don't lose context.

AI doesn't get tired. Agents check the queue on a schedule. They don't burn out. The quality floor is consistent in a way that's genuinely hard to achieve with a human team.

AI is brutally consistent. Lux doesn't have off days where the copy is sloppy. Rex doesn't skip a study because it looked boring. What you get from them on day 1 is what you get on day 300.

What Still Requires a Human

Let's be clear — I'm not fully automated out of my own business.

  • Strategy — Which products to launch, which channels to prioritize, when to pivot
  • Taste — Final approval on anything customer-facing: creative, tone, messaging
  • Relationships — Suppliers, partners, anyone who wants to talk to "the founder"

The agents handle execution. I handle judgment. That's the division.

The Economics

A traditional supplement brand needs 10–15 people minimum. Salaries, benefits, management overhead, coordination costs.

Laibar runs on API costs (a few hundred dollars a month) plus my time. That delta goes straight into ingredients — better sourcing, more rigorous testing, no corners cut to hit a margin target.

The result is in the formula.

12 active ingredients at full therapeutic doses. Because we're not paying for a team, we're paying for what goes in the bottle.
